3 Things That Will Make or Break Your Paper
🔍
Lead with the data leakage finding
Your single strongest original contribution is identifying the Reuters source-tag leakage in ISOT. Dedicate a full sub-section to it. Show the inflated 99% vs honest 88–93% accuracy side-by-side. This is what no other student paper in your class will have.
📄
Use the IEEE template from Day 1
Download the IEEE double-column Word or LaTeX template before you write a single word. Reformatting at the end wastes an entire day. Search "IEEE conference template" — it's free. Overleaf is the easiest option if you use LaTeX.
✍️
Write the Abstract last (Day 11)
You can only write a good abstract after you know your results. Write it last. It should be 150–200 words and answer: what problem, what method, what dataset, what accuracy. Every sentence in the abstract must be backed by something in the paper body.
